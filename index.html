Anonymity and Art

Artists should be anonymous not because it doesn't matter who the artist is (with a few exceptions), but because it allows both the artist and the audience to be real. The anonymous artist is free to express themselves without regard for success or failure, and they cannot question their own motives.

We must not confuse art with artists. We must focus our attention on the art itself and judge it according to our own aesthetic sensibilities, which we must constantly develop by seeking out art where it is hidden or overshadowed; choosing what to be exposed to rather than passively allowing ourselves to be exposed to whatever is front and centre. As many of us are aware, it is not where the best art is found. This is our way of thinking.
